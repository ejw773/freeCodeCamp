Alfred Hitchcock

The Master of Suspense

Here are some of Hitchcock's most notable films:

  • 1935 - The 39 Steps is his earliest film to receive universal acclaim.
  • 1940 - Rebecca was his only Best Picture Oscar winner.
  • 1954 - Rear Window was initially ignored by American films critics, but adored by critics in Europe.
  • 1958 - Vertigo is recognized as his best film, and is listed as the #1 film of all time by the British Film Institute.
  • 1959 - North by Northwest features a story structure that was a forerunner to James Bond and modern action films.
  • 1960 - Psycho, featuring an amazing score by Bernard Herrmann, is his most frightening film.
  • 1962 - The Birds was his last big success before his years of decline.

    • "In examining his films, it was obvious that he had given more thought to the potential of his art than any of his colleagues."
